3D Printed Dremel

I 3D printed a model of a dremel body using Ultimaker Cura to house the motor and wire controls. I connected power supply wires to an adjustable motor controller (for the speed of the rotary tool) and soldered wires from the controller to the DC motor. Then, using a rotary tool kit (drill chucks, drill collets, drill bits, etc.), I mounted drilling tools to the motor shaft to complete the dremel.

Project information

  • Category: Hardware
  • Date(s): June 2020
Components
  • Dremel STL files
  • PWM Motor Speed Controller x1
    • The controller was connected to the power supply port and the motor for the user to be able to adjust the motor speed.
  • 12V 2A Power Supply x1
    • The power supply port was connected to the motor controller to provide power to the dremel.
  • 30AWG Wires
    • Used about 1 ft of each of the red and black wires.
  • 12V 18000RPM DC Motor x1
    • The motor provides the rotary functionality of the dremel and its speed is controller by the PWM motor controller.
  • Solder | Soldering Iron | Iron Holder
    • Used to solder the wires to the panels and the USB converter.
